## Executive Summary
This project analyzes pizza sales data using MySQL to uncover insights about customer ordering patterns, product performance, and revenue trends. By applying SQL queries ranging from basic aggregations to advanced analytical techniques, the project demonstrates how raw transactional data can be transformed into meaningful business insights to support data-driven decision-making.

## Project Overview
The goal of this project is to explore pizza sales data and answer real-world business questions related to sales volume, revenue contribution, popular pizza types, and time-based ordering patterns. The analysis progresses from basic metrics to intermediate and advanced insights, showcasing the practical application of SQL in solving business problems.

## Business Questions Answered

### Basic Analysis
- Retrieve the total number of orders placed  
- Calculate the total revenue generated from pizza sales  
- Identify the highest-priced pizza  
- Identify the most common pizza size ordered  
- List the top 5 most ordered pizza types along with their quantities  

### Intermediate Analysis
- Find the total quantity of pizzas ordered by category  
- Analyze the distribution of orders by hour of the day  
- Determine category-wise pizza distribution  
- Calculate the average number of pizzas ordered per day  
- Identify the top 3 pizza types based on revenue  

### Advanced Analysis
- Calculate the percentage contribution of each pizza type to total revenue  
- Analyze cumulative revenue generated over time  
- Identify the top 3 pizzas by revenue within each pizza category  

---

## Key Insights
- Large-sized pizzas contribute the highest share of total revenue, indicating a preference for larger portions  
- Order volume peaks in the evening hours, particularly between 6 PM and 8 PM  
- A small number of pizza types generate a significant portion of total revenue  
- Classic category pizzas are the most frequently ordered  
- Cumulative revenue shows steady growth over time  
- Top-performing pizzas vary across categories, highlighting the importance of category-level analysis
